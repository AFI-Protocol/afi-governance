## 2. D-ATLAS-1 â€” Atlas purpose

**Decision.** The AFI District / API Atlas is the protocol's **canonical discoverability and relationship layer**: it records what protocol capabilities, authority domains (Districts), structures, interfaces, routes, contracts, repositories, participant roles, and maturity/visibility exist and how they relate. It is a **description of truth, not a source of it**. It does **not** replace, override, or re-decide runtime behavior, canonical schemas/contracts, or governance authority; where the Atlas and any higher authority (an accepted decision, a governed contract, current implementation) disagree, the higher authority wins and the Atlas is the surface that must be corrected. This designation is the "designation of a canonical API Atlas" that `authority-districts-v0.1.md` Part E.1 reserves to governance, and it fills the `ATLAS-GOV` slot Part H, the District-2 ratification Â§7, and D1CAP-GOV D-D1CAP-8 each deferred to it. Upon owner merge it **supersedes, prospectively and as a governance-ledger fact, the D-D1CAP-8 item-2 standing statement "No canonical API Atlas exists"** â€” a canonical API Atlas foundation (v0.1) now exists â€” without rewriting that file.

**Scope-guard.** Discoverability designation only; grants the Atlas no execution, routing, resolution, or authority power, and no District/contract/runtime change.

## 3. D-ATLAS-2 â€” District identity

**Decision.** A District is an **enduring capability and authority domain**, independent of repositories and of any particular replaceable implementation. A District may span multiple repositories, contain multiple capabilities, be served by multiple structures, and emit/receive multiple typed routes. The Atlas records the two active Districts exactly as accepted governance defines them â€” **District One = Signal Evaluation** (D1CAP-GOV D-D1CAP-1/2) and **District Two = Evidence and Provenance** (D1CAP-GOV D-D1CAP-8(1) normative terminology; `Canonical Data & Provenance Boundary` remains its registry long-form per `authority-districts-v0.1.md` Part D) â€” and creates no other District. Districts are not renamed, re-scoped, or re-numbered by this decision. The future-district candidates that `authority-districts-v0.1.md` Part D.2 already records as unnumbered and uncreated (including the settlement/rewards/on-chain domain) are represented in the Atlas registry as **reserved capability-domain space** (`active: false`, maturity `reserved`), never as active or numbered Districts.

**Scope-guard.** Identity-model record; consumes AD/DSC-GOV/D1CAP-GOV/District-2 ratification; creates or changes no District.

## 4. D-ATLAS-3 â€” Structure identity

**Decision.** A **Structure** is a concrete AFI system, service, facility, or shared infrastructure (e.g. the Reactor runtime, the afi-core scoring library, the afi-math kernels, the Factory authoring system, the Gateway, the Tiny Brains service, the afi-infra evidence store, the afi-config registries, the governance ledger, the on-chain token). A Structure may have a home District, serve several Districts, sit at a District boundary, route traffic without owning the routed capability, or remain unassigned while the owning authority is unresolved. **A Structure is not automatically a District.** No "Core District," "Reactor District," "Factory District," or "Gateway District" is created merely because those structures or repositories exist. The Reactor runtime, for example, serves both District One (execution) and District Two (Evidence V3 construction) without being either District; the Gateway serves District One by routing without owning District One's capability.

**Scope-guard.** Ontology record; assigns no new authority to any structure.

## 5. D-ATLAS-4 â€” Interface ownership vs. routing

**Decision.** An interface is a governed I/O boundary for a capability. **The interface contract belongs to the capability and the District that governs that capability.** A Gateway or other routing structure may authenticate, authorize, rate-limit, translate transports, aggregate, and route to an interface **without acquiring ownership** of the underlying capability or its contract. The canonical relationship is: *District owns capability â†’ capability owns interface contract â†’ Gateway may expose or route to that interface.* The afi-gateway `POST /api/v1/signals` boundary routes to District One's signal-evaluation capability; it does not own that capability (consistent with `authority-districts-v0.1.md` Part C's "afi-gateway holds no unilateral domain-schema authority", INST-GOV's Gateway classification, and the gateway's own routes-not-writes guardrail tests). The Atlas records `exposingStructure` and `routingStructure` separately from the owning capability/District.

**Scope-guard.** Ownership-model record; changes no route, no Gateway behavior, and no interface implementation.

## 6. D-ATLAS-5 â€” Typed routes

**Decision.** Every route in the Atlas MUST name its **flow type**. The canonical flow-type set is: `data`, `control`, `identityTrust`, `evidence`, `buildArtifact`, `value`. A route MUST NOT imply network transport when the boundary is an in-process function call or a schema handoff (the Atlas records whether a route is a network boundary; e.g. the District One â†’ District Two evidence handoff is an in-process typed submission, not a network route). Additional flow types may be added only when current implementation evidence requires one and a new Atlas registry version records it (D-ATLAS-14).

**Scope-guard.** Route-typing rule; draws no new route and creates no transport.

## 7. D-ATLAS-6 â€” Honest maturity, separated from visibility

**Decision.** Atlas records MUST carry **two independent axes**. **Maturity** âˆˆ {`operational`, `partial`, `underConstruction`, `planned`, `reserved`}. **Visibility** âˆˆ {`public`, `restricted`, `internal`}. Maturity is a lifecycle/implementation fact; visibility is an access/exposure fact; neither implies the other (a capability may be `operational` yet `internal`, since the organization-wide posture remains non-production â€” consistent with D1CAP-GOV D-D1CAP-1's "active is a capability statement, not a deployment statement"). An entity may be labeled **`operational` only with direct implementation and current-test/verification evidence** cited in its evidence references. `partial` requires a meaningful current implementation with the incomplete surfaces named (e.g. the afi-token testnet surface). `underConstruction`/`planned`/`reserved` make no claim of operational access.

**Scope-guard.** Status-semantics rule; asserts no status about any entity by itself (each entity's status is a registry fact backed by cited evidence).

## 8. D-ATLAS-7 â€” No invented APIs

**Decision.** An interface may be marked `operational` **only when a real implemented surface supports it**, verified by a current path and test. Planned, under-construction, or reserved interfaces MUST NOT contain fabricated route strings, URLs, credentials, hostnames, throughput figures, or operational guarantees. A non-operational interface describes its intended capability at a bounded level and names what remains incomplete. The Atlas registry's schema structurally forbids an address/operation identifier on a non-operational interface and forbids any secret-capable field on any entity (D-ATLAS-9).

**Scope-guard.** Honesty rule; creates no endpoint and authorizes no exposure.

## 9. D-ATLAS-8 â€” Repository relationship

**Decision.** Repositories are **implementation references**, not District definitions. A repository may implement one District, support several Districts, host shared infrastructure, or hold no District-defining authority at all; a District may span several repositories (District Two spans `afi-reactor`, `afi-infra`, and `afi-config`). **No District is defined solely by a repository name, and no one-to-one Districtâ†”repository rule exists.** The Atlas registry records repositories with their current role and the capabilities/structures they implement or host, and the registry's guards forbid a District whose identity is a bare repository name.

**Scope-guard.** Relationship record; changes no repository's role or authority.

## 15. D-ATLAS-14 â€” Versioning

**Decision.** The Atlas registry is versioned as the contract `afi.protocol-atlas.v1` with an internal `registryVersion`. Rules:
- **Entity-ID stability.** Entity IDs (`districtId`, `structureId`, `capabilityId`, `interfaceId`, `routeId`, contract/repository/role/onboarding IDs) are stable identifiers; once published they are not reused for a different entity and are not renamed to track an implementation change.
- **Implementation independence.** A structure or implementation may change (a repository may be replaced, a service rewritten) **without renaming the District or capability it implements** â€” the evidence references update, the identity does not (consistent with D1CAP-GOV D-D1CAP-5's replacement rule).
- **Additive vs. breaking.** Adding an entity, an evidence reference, or a permitted-enum member consistent with Â§Â§2â€“8 is **additive** (same major version). Changing an entity's identity meaning, removing a required field, or altering an invariant is **breaking** and requires a new Atlas contract major version and a governance decision.
- **Status transitions** follow the maturity ladder honestly (`reserved`â†’`planned`â†’`underConstruction`â†’`partial`â†’`operational`) only as real implementation/verification evidence accrues; a status may regress if evidence is withdrawn. Every `operational`/`partial` transition MUST cite current evidence.
- **Removal policy (forward-only).** A removed entity is deleted forward-only from the current registry (no compatibility alias, no archive copy, no tombstone kept current); Git history is the archive â€” consistent with owner doctrine and EV3-GOV D-EV3-8's forward-only precedent.
- **Evidence-reference maintenance.** Evidence references are kept current with the trees they cite; a stale evidence reference is a drift-guard failure, not a silent inaccuracy.

**Scope-guard.** Versioning/lifecycle rule for the Atlas registry only; governs no other contract's versioning.
